  ### Advanced Macroeconomic Analysis II *(PhD/MA, summer term 2025)*

  This course uses the recent macroeconomic experience — from the pandemic stimulus and the build-up of public debt, to the surge in energy prices after the invasion of Ukraine and the subsequent monetary tightening — as a lens through which to study the theory and empirics of monetary and fiscal policy. We identify monetary and fiscal policy shocks, discuss their transmission in the economy, and analyse how the two policy domains interact. By the end of the course, students understand why policymakers have reacted as they have in recent years and what the theoretical and empirical toolkit of modern macroeconomics says about those reactions.

  ### Seminar on Monetary Theory *(BA, winter term 2024/25, taught in German)*

  This seminar asks three deceptively simple questions: What turns a piece of paper or digital bits into money? Why is money valuable? And how is its value determined? To answer them, the seminar introduces and compares three theories of the value of money — the Quantity Theory, the Fiscal Theory of the Price Level, and Modern Monetary Theory. Each theory is presented by a student team and then tested against historical episodes of high and low inflation, from the German hyperinflation of the 1920s and the Great Depression to the zero lower bound after the financial crisis and the 2022 inflation surge. The seminar concludes with a synthesis that asks how well each theory helps us understand the data.
