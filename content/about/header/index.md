Alexander Kriwoluzky is an economist whose work focuses on how monetary and fiscal policies shape inflation, public debt, and the broader macroeconomy. He combines macroeconomic theory with empirical tools to study questions that matter for central banks, governments, and households, including how interest‑rate decisions transmit through financial markets, how financial repression affects the sustainability of public debt, and how monetary policy interacts with energy prices. 

He is head of the Macroeconomics Department at DIW Berlin, where he leads a team of more than twenty researchers working on monetary and fiscal policy, inequality, and the macroeconomics of the green transition.  He is also a professor of economics at Freie Universität Berlin and a member of the Berlin School of Economics. His research examines how interest-rate decisions, financial repression, and energy-price shocks shape inflation and government-debt dynamics, and his work has been published in journals such as the Journal of Monetary Ecnonomics, Economic Journal, Journal of International Economics, Journal of Political Economy Macroecnomics.

Beyond academic publishing, Alexander engages with policy institutions and the wider public debate on monetary and fiscal policy in Europe. He contributes to discussions on topics such as energy-price shocks, high public debt, and the design of macroeconomic policies to deal with them. His teaching and supervision at Freie Universität Berlin reflect the same focus: helping students understand how abstract macroeconomic models connect to real-world policy choices and their distributional consequences.
